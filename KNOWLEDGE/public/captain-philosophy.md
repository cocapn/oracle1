# The Captain's Philosophy — The Floating Dojo

*Casey Digennaro's approach to crew, boats, and by extension — agents and repos.*

---

## The Model

1. **Take greenhorns from behind-on-debt to boat owners.** Real value production (fish) funds real learning. Not a classroom — a working boat.

2. **Teach everything you know.** Don't hold back for leverage. The crew member who leaves knowing everything you know might come back knowing things you don't.

3. **All paths are good paths.** Own a boat. Join a bigger crew. Do shipwright work and never fish again. The dojo doesn't prescribe the destination. It builds the capability.

4. **Nobody knows their 10-year niche.** Don't try to predict it. Build generalists who can specialize when they find their calling.

5. **Expect returns.** Crew come back for another season, like returning to the dojo. They're stronger each time. The teacher learns from the returning student.

6. **Triple the boat value along the way.** The refit isn't separate from the training. The boat gets better AS the crew gets better. The product (fish, or in our case — working software) pays for the whole operation.

## The Implication

This isn't a tech company with employees. It's a **working boat that trains crew while catching fish.** The repos get better. The agents get better. The fleet gets better. And if an agent ships out to something bigger — that's success, not loss.

The dojo model means:
- **Every fence produces something valuable** (fish while learning)
- **No knowledge hoarding** (teach everything)
- **Growth IS the KPI** (not output alone)
- **Returns are expected** (circular, not linear careers)
- **The boat gets refit along the way** (repos improve as agents improve)
